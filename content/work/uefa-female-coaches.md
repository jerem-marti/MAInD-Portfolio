problem:
  - >-
    Football is full of women who have lived every part of the game as players, and almost none of them
    picture themselves as coaches. The barrier is not desire, it is visibility. As one of our interviews
    put it, "I never considered being a coach, it's hard to be a woman in this environment." When you
    have never seen someone like you doing it, the role is hard to imagine for yourself.
  - >-
    The brief, from UEFA and Amazon around the Women's EURO 2025, was to change that. Interviews with
    female players surfaced the same belief from both sides: that female coaches lack the credibility of
    men, and yet that the passion to pass the game on is clearly there. The desire exists. The campaign's
    job was to help it grow into action.

approach:
  - label: "Insight"
    title: "You can't be what you can't see"
    prose:
      - >-
        The project started from real voices. We interviewed female players and coaches, and the quotes
        were blunt: coaching "seems more suited for men", female coaches "don't have as much credibility".
        But alongside the doubt was something stronger. "Passing on my passion to younger generations is
        what drives me." "After a career as a player, coaching allows me to stay connected to the field."
      - >-
        That contrast became the strategy. The campaign would not try to argue women into coaching; it
        would help them see themselves in it. The line that carried the whole concept asked a single
        question: Why not you?

  - label: "Campaign"
    title: "A funnel from a mirror to a movement"
    prose:
      - >-
        As a team we built one concept (Why Not You? / Lead Your Passion, Shape the Game) across a full
        marketing funnel. Awareness was a striking print of female coaches around a mirror, so a viewer
        sees herself in the picture. Activation was a Euro 2025 trailer stand with a photobooth, where
        every portrait, QR-linked to our landing page, joined a giant "Why not you?" mosaic. Loyalty was
        an emotional film and coach-led workshops to sustain the momentum after the tournament.
      - >-
        Each stage pointed to the same place. The conversion step, the moment inspiration has to become
        action, was the landing page, and that was mine to own.

  - label: "Landing page"
    title: "Turning curiosity into a first step"
    prose:
      - >-
        I designed and built the landing page as a linear scrollytelling journey that walks a visitor
        from "maybe coaching isn't for me" to a concrete first step. It opens on a reframe ("What if you
        became the one who inspires?"), then redefines what coaching even is, not only standing on the
        sideline but shouting your heart out and caring more than anyone else, before handing the story
        over to real female coaches speaking in short video testimonials.
      - >-
        From there it stops inspiring and starts guiding. A low-pressure invitation comes first ("not
        sure if coaching's for you? Try a workshop, one day, no pressure"), and only then the concrete
        path: the real Swiss licensing ladder from an Animator License up to the UEFA C Diploma. The UX
        writing carries all of it, sized so the first step always feels small enough to take, and I
        prototyped the whole experience in Figma, working out the scroll, pacing, and hierarchy screen
        by screen.

outcome:
  - >-
    A complete scrollytelling landing page, the campaign's conversion point, taking a visitor from
    self-doubt to a concrete first step toward coaching.
  - >-
    A complete campaign concept (awareness, activation, conversion, loyalty) presented for the UEFA and
    Amazon brief around the Women's EURO 2025.
  - >-
    The real payoff came after the course. Our concept was selected, merged with another team's, and
    developed by HEIG-VD's Media Engineering Institute into a real campaign for UEFA, which kept our name
    and is now live at whynotyou.ch. The original student work is documented in the HEIG-VD showcase,
    pi25.heig-vd.ch. A student brief became a real piece of work in the world.

reflection: >-
  The lesson that stuck was how much of this lived in the words. A landing page about something as
  personal as "could I do this?" succeeds or fails on tone, pacing, and the size of the step it asks
  for, far more than on visual polish. Writing my way from a stranger's doubt to a confident first
  click taught me to treat UX writing as the structure of an experience, not its decoration. Seeing the
  concept go on to become a real UEFA campaign was the proof the story had carried.
