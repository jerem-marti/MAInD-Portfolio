01

The insight & the reconnection promise

The allergy category runs on one convention — measure the data, then notify. It leaves people either blindsided or hypervigilant, and both stop trusting their own body.

Marco

The beginner, blindsided — the trigger is invisible, the reaction arrives with no warning.

Mary

The expert, hypervigilant — she knows too much, and the constant alerts have become noise.

Both

They've stopped trusting their own body to tell them where the limit is.

My body keeps the truth from me until it's too late — so I'm stuck between being caught off guard and living on permanent alert. I want to feel it coming, quietly, while the choice to act is still mine.

The turn. Calm perception is only the means. The end is your life back. Where every allergy product says avoid, protect, hide, Thea says come back — sense the world calmly enough to step into it again.

02

The platform

Purpose

Awareness should feel like a sense, not an alarm. Everyone deserves to feel their own limits — calmly, early, on their own terms.

Mission

Turn invisible allergen load into a sense you can feel, early enough that the choice stays yours — so you can reconnect with the world you've been avoiding.

Vision

A world where your limits are something you sense, not something that ambushes you — and where sensing them gives you the world back.

Values

  • Calm by default, clear when it countsA whisper over a shout — but plain at the true edge. The rarity is the feature.
  • Perception first, evidence on requestThe sense never shows a number; understanding is pulled, never pushed.
  • Yours aloneYour body, your data, your pace. Local and private.
  • Trust earned, not demandedAccuracy and restraint — disbelief to quiet mastery.
  • Meet you where you areFrom Marco's first discovery to Mary's worn-out expertise.

Our combat: against alarm culture — the belief that more alerts mean more care, the false trade-off between blindsided and anxious, and the body reduced to a dashboard.

04

Verbal identity

Name

Thea — product, agent and brand in one. From Greek θeá (goddess) and Theia, Titaness of sight and light.

Signature

The sense you were missing.

Hero line

Reconnect with your allergens. Benefit: get your life back.

Tone of voice

  • Say less — silence is part of the voice.
  • Plain, not clinical — no spoken numbers or jargon.
  • Warm, never anxious — the voice itself is never scared.
  • Honest at the edge — plain and direct at the critical moment.
  • Respects your agency — informs and trusts; never nags.
Calm — default

"Pollen's building this morning. Nothing urgent — I'll keep watch."

"You've got plenty of headroom today — go."

Caring-critical — rare

"You're right at your edge now. I'd head inside."

"This is the moment — time to take something."

Reconnection — opens the door

"The count's low today. Good morning to be out."

Encourages, never pushes — the choice is always yours.

Never this: "Alert: allergen level critical!" · "Pollen index 8.2 (very high)"

Orange, two registers. Orange means "now" only on the live loop. Everywhere else — packaging, type, the lifeline motif, the reconnection campaign — it's free, static brand energy, never alarm. Separation is by surface and motion, not a second colour. Orange is warm, not red: a caring "this matters," never a clinical emergency.

Motion

Idle · listening · thinking · speaking · critical · resting — calm, never bouncing. Glowworm calm, firefly alert (firm not frantic).

06

Accessibility — WCAG AA is the floor

Every text pairing meets WCAG 2.1 AA at minimum: ≥ 4.5:1 for normal text, ≥ 3:1 for large (≥ 24px or 19px bold) and UI. Verified from this palette:

  • 14.9 Ink on linen body ✓
  • 5.5 Ink on orange — dark text is how you label an orange fill body ✓
  • 7.5 Cobalt Deep on white — small blue text & links body ✓
  • 4.6 White on Cobalt body ✓
  • 3.8 Cobalt on linen — headings only large only
  • 3.3 White on Orange — display only; for body use Ink large only
  • 2.7 Orange text on linen — don't; orange is a fill avoid
  • 2.9 White on Sky — don't; Sky takes dark text avoid

Defaults: body text is Ink; small blue text and links are Cobalt Deep; orange fills carry dark (Ink) text; Sky and Orange are tints and fills, not text colours on light.

08

Do & don't

Do

  • Keep Thea a presence that implies sight, never a face
  • On the loop: white rests, cobalt speaks, orange acts
  • Use orange freely as static brand energy, the lifeline
  • Point outward — toward reconnection and getting the world back
  • Meet WCAG AA as a floor on every surface

Don't

  • Add a literal eye, face, or central gaze point to the live avatar
  • Use orange as a live signal anywhere but the loop
  • Show numbers as the primary signal, or build a real-time load meter
  • Make Thea anxious, naggy, or alarmist
  • Ship any text below AA contrast (no orange body text on linen, no white on sky)
Branding→Personality→System prompt

The brand is the source. Thea's personality and system prompt derive from this book — the platform becomes her character, the tone principles her rules, the registers her voice. Nothing downstream contradicts what's written here.
